Hello, and welcome to my portfolio!

My name is Jackson Luff. I'm a 19 year old game developer that has developed in several different languages; C++, C#, JavaScript, HTML and CSS - To name a few. I am currently undergoing the Advanced Diploma of Professional Game Development at Academy of Interactive Entertainment (AIE). My ambition fuelling most of my projects is a clash of creative chaos, the unique beauty and power of computer graphics, and of course, the notorious lack of sleep.

Here you'll find all kinds of weird stuff to do about me. If you're interested in weird/wacky things that I too enjoy but have no relation to me, visit my blog. That's the place where i talk mostly about on-going updates to GenGein [my beast of an engine] or just qwerky/fascinating stuff!

If you are in fact here to query a brief description of myself and what i do, continue reading! ... Or not. That choice is completely dependant on if you want a good programmer or not.

As you probably presumed, computer graphics is indeed the area that I enjoy adventuring the most. Whether it be CPU or GPU - there is always a way to make your project be pretty. As you will see throughout my projects, I thrive to post eye-catching content [e.g. this portfolio]. This is for two reasons. The first being that it's hard to please everyone. But it's easy to gain a larger audience with a pretty persona than with "programmer graphics". The second reason is self-assurance. There is no better feeling than to overview your work as if it were an art gallery.

Now, programming for myself isn't just about sitting at a computer tweaking values in ugly code hoping for a result. No, Beautiful programming is my basis. What I attempt to produce are projects that retain readability and both efficiency and effectiveness. How, you say? Well first the main problem present is that as the project expands, so does the probability for your project to become a plate full of spaghetti and hope. Thus, I often go through my code to clean up what should be where and fix up the execution structure to ensure the code is more optimal than previously. This process is often overlooked by the client, but the passion is still visible.
